LEARNINGS / DEVIATIONS FROM PLAN (all are test-expectation fixes; implementations match the spec)
1. Plan's test_quality_company_passes used shares=1000 -> $50K market cap, which correctly FAILS the spec's >$1B rule. Fixed the synthetic fixture to 1e8 shares (100M) so the real $1B default threshold is exercised. Same fix applied to test_integration's PASS/DILUTE fixtures (1e8 / 3e8).
2. Plan's test_cagr_from_value tolerance 1e-6 too tight: calendar-day annualization (3653 days/365.25 ~ 10.0014y) differs from exact-10y by ~1e-5. Loosened to 1e-4.
3. Plan's test_integration used as_of=date(2015,1,2): the synthetic FY2014 10-K is "filed" 2015-02-15, so at Jan 2 only 10 FYs (2004-2013) are available — one short of the 11 the 10y lookback needs, so NOTHING passes. This is CORRECT point-in-time behavior. Changed as_of to date(2015,6,1) (after FY2014 filings) to demonstrate a passer.
4. Added .cache/ to root .gitignore for the parquet price cache.

KEY POINT-IN-TIME INSIGHT FOR THE LIVE RUN (US-009)
- The 10y lookback needs 11 filed fiscal years. SEC XBRL companyfacts generally only goes back to ~2009 (XBRL mandate). So early rebalance years (2015-2019) will have MANY names dropped for insufficient filed history; coverage should improve toward 2020+. Expect a high "dropped" count and possibly few/empty holdings in early years. This is an HONEST limitation to report, NOT something to engineer around.
- At rebalance as_of=Jan 1 of year T, the prior-year (T-1) 10-K typically isn't filed yet (filed Feb-Mar), so the screen effectively uses FYs through T-2. Correct point-in-time.
